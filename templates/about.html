About the Application

The Model Concept application was developed to experiment with a web-based approach to health economic modeling. The data presented represents a simple Markov model designed to show-case some of the benefits of using a web based design. Some of these include:
  • Ability to share structure and results more easily and to potentially larger audiences

  • Advantages associated with use of various html elements (e.g., tooltips and links) to more fully integrate the data source documentation with model structure and user modifiable data inputs

  • Capacity to use the web as a portal to provide/collect feedback on design and assumptions more directly compared with excel based designs

About Markov Models

Markov models are used to represent stochastic processes and are frequently used in health economic evaluations, usually to model chronic diseases. In a Markov model, the disease under study is divided into distinct states with transition probabilities used to describe the likelihood of moving from one state to the next over a fixed period of time (usually 6-month or 1-year periods). Long term outcomes and costs associated with a disease can then be estimated by attaching unit costs and health effects to states in the model and running the model over a large number of cycles.
