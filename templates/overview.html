Overview of Structure

In common with previous studies, the cardiovascular disease (CVD) model has 5 disease states: Healthy, Myocardial infarction (MI), Stroke, CVD death, and death due to other causes. A Markov model is used to calculate the proportion of patients in each of the disease states during each yearly cycle for each treatment of interest (Drug A vs. Drug B) as well as costs and utilities associated with each state.

All patients begin in the Healthy state.

Patients are at risk for subsequent CVD events including MI and stroke.

Patients who experience either an MI or stroke are at risk of dying due to CVD.

Patients may also die from other (non-CVD) causes.

Patients may experience different events and transition between MI, Stroke and Healthy states prior to death.
